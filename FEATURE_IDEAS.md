## THE THESIS

Every feature below passes one test: **"Can Google do this?"**
If yes ‚Üí skip it. If no ‚Üí that's our lane.

Google has the map, the reviews, the data. We don't compete on static info.
We compete on **live, ephemeral, human-sourced intelligence that expires**.

### 3. "Going There" Intent Signals
**What**: User taps "Heading there" on any venue. Others see: "4 people heading to HEB in the next hour"
**Why it's different**: Nobody does this. It creates FOMO ("oh, everyone's going to the farmers market"), helps avoid crowds ("12 people heading to Costco, maybe wait"), and builds community momentum.
**UX**: One-tap "üöó Heading there" button on any Local listing. Shows count. Auto-clears after 2h.

**What**: Local businesses push 30-min to 2-hour deals: "Free cookie with any coffee ‚Äî next 45 min only" or "Happy hour started early, $3 margs until 5"
**Why it's different**: Groupon is dead. Yelp deals are stale. These are LIVE, EXPIRING, and from businesses in YOUR radius. Creates urgency and foot traffic.

**Revenue potential**: This is the monetization play. Free for first 5 drops/month, then $10/month for businesses.
**Killer detail**: "‚òï Stockyard Coffee just dropped a deal ‚Äî 0.3 mi away, expires in 22 min"

### 5. "Ask the Block" (Ephemeral Local Q&A)
**What**: Ask a question, get answers from people within your radius. "Anyone know a good plumber?" "What time does the food truck on 1431 show up?" Questions expire in 24h.
**Why it's different**: Google reviews are permanent, SEO-gamed, and from 2019. Reddit is city-wide. Nextdoor is slow. This is FRESH, LOCAL, and from your actual neighbors.
**UX**: Separate tab or section in Pulse. Questions get "helpful" upvotes. Best answer pinned.
**Killer detail**: Answers from "Local Hero" badge holders get highlighted ‚Äî community trust system pays off here.
